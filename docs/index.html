The Acts of the Apostles

Caitlin Murphy, Gabrielle Reyes, Nathaniel Qin, and Winson Luk

About

The Acts of the Apostles chronicles the ministry of the apostles, mainly Peter and Paul, throughout Jerusalem, Samaria, and the Roman Empire.

  • Focus

    Acts focuses on the spread of the gospel from a Jewish audience to both Jewish and Gentile audiences, extending themes of salvation through repentance to include all people from even “the remotest part of the earth” (1:8)

  • Similarity with Luke

    Written between 80-90, Acts is credited to the same author as Luke and contains many similar themes, including the fulfillment of God’s promises through ministry and the absolute reliability of God’s word.

  • Paul in Acts

    It is important to understand there is a distinction between Paul himself and Paul the character as interpreted by the author of Acts (Bruce 283-305).

Journey

People

Paul

Though he initially persecutes the church, after receiving a message from God, Paul becomes an apostle heavily credited with the ministry of Christianity to Gentiles.

Ananias and Sapphira

Ananais and Sapphira succumb to sin by lying and giving only a portion of their earnings to the community. Although they are members of the church, they are not impervious to sin.

Stephen

After Stephen preaches to the council, the crowd is angered and frenzied, leading them to stone him. Still, he asks “Lord, do not hold this sin against them” and accepts his death.(7:60)

Philip

A waiter who preaches in Samaria. Later baptizes the Ethiopian eunuch.

Peter

Performs many miracles, like raising a widow from the dead and healing the ill. Saw the sheet of animals which led to the inclusivity of Gentiles. Teaches along with Paul.

Cornelius

He was a Gentile who was devoted to God and was told by an angel of God to send for Peter. Peter then came and told the story of Jesus to them, and the Holy Spirit came upon Cornelius, his relatives, and his close friends.
